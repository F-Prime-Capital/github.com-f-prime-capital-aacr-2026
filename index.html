Executive Summary

Six Signals That Matter

AACR measures what is being built, not what has moved. These six patterns represent the structural forces shaping the oncology pipeline over the next three to five years.

01

AI Is the Default Research Method Now

362 abstracts explicitly reference machine learning, deep learning, or AI methods. This is no longer a subfield. It is the standard toolkit for target identification, image analysis, biomarker discovery, and drug design. The companies with native-AI discovery stacks will have a structural speed advantage in the next cycle. "We use AI" no longer differentiates anything.

11% of abstracts explicitly reference AI or ML methods
02

ADC Target Diversification Is Accelerating

HER2 still leads ADC research with 59 co-mentions, but TROP2, HER3, B7-H3, DLL3, CLDN18.2, and CDH17 are each building critical mass. The ADC format has become modular. The warhead question is largely answered. The race now is to the best target.

03

KRAS Has Caught HER2 in Research Volume

155 abstracts identify KRAS as a primary target — nearly tied with HER2 (154) and EGFR (149). G12D programs now outnumber G12C in early research. Revolution Medicines' pan-RAS approach casts a shadow over allele-specific bets. The field is converging on pan-RAS, not allele-specific, as the long-term standard.

Target landscape →
04

The TME Is an Active Target Space

385 abstracts directly address the tumor microenvironment. The consensus has shifted: the TME is not just a barrier to therapy, it is a target. Myeloid reprogramming, stromal remodeling, and spatial biology (159 abstracts) are the active frontiers, not incremental checkpoint additions.

Emerging signals →
05

Degraders Are Finding Their Footing

72 PROTAC and degrader abstracts — small but maturing fast. KRAS (13 mentions) and BRD4 (6) lead as degrader targets. The DAC format, a degrader payload delivered by antibody conjugate, is the most structurally novel concept at AACR 2026. It solves PROTAC cell permeability by using the antibody for targeting.

06

Solid Tumors Are Finding New Surface Antigens

The hard limit on ADC, bispecific, and CAR-T programs in solid tumors has always been target quality. AACR 2026 shows the field is making progress. B7-H3 (35 abstracts), PSMA (20), DLL3 (23), CDH17 (10), and CLDN18.2 (15) are each crossing the threshold where modality convergence begins — multiple drug formats aimed at the same target in the same indication. That is what happened with CD19 in lymphoma before the cell therapy wave hit.

What Moves Conviction

▲

Strengthen Position

  • ADC platform companies with payload or linker differentiation
  • Pan-RAS small molecule programs with G12D and G12V coverage
  • AI-native target discovery integrating multi-omic data
  • B7-H3 and DLL3 programs in solid tumors across modalities
  • CDH17 as a GI-specific target before it gets crowded
→

Monitor Closely

  • PROTAC platforms targeting KRAS — degradation may beat inhibition
  • Spatial biology infrastructure and TME profiling tools
  • mRNA/LNP programs beyond vaccines — in vivo CAR-T and cytokine delivery
  • Radiopharmaceutical expansion beyond PSMA-prostate
  • Bispecific ADC format — early data is exceptional
▼

Exercise Caution

  • Me-too PD-1/PD-L1 programs — the market has been saturated
  • G12C-only KRAS inhibitors — pan-RAS will likely disintermediate
  • CAR-T in heme without differentiated manufacturing cost structure
  • TIGIT, IDO, and the broader next-gen checkpoint graveyard
