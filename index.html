Executive Summary

Six Signals That Matter

AACR 2026 is a window into the pipeline, not the clinic. Unlike ASCO — which measures what has moved — AACR measures what is being built. With 3,489 abstracts processed by AI agents, these are the structural signals a life sciences investor needs to understand.

01

AI Has Colonized Basic Research

2,277 abstracts mention machine learning, deep learning, or AI — that's 65% of all presentations. This isn't "AI in oncology" as a vertical anymore. It's the default methodology for target identification, image analysis, biomarker discovery, and drug design. The signal: companies that are native-AI in their discovery stack will have a structural speed advantage in the next cycle.

65% of abstracts mention AI/ML methods
02

ADC Target Diversification Accelerates

HER2 remains dominant in ADC research (59 co-mentions), but TROP-2, HER3, B7-H3, DLL3, CLDN18.2, and CDH17 are each building critical mass. The ADC format is becoming modular — the warhead is solved, now the race is to the best target.

03

KRAS: From Undruggable to Crowded

170 abstracts mention KRAS, with G12D programs now overtaking G12C in early research volume. Revolution Medicines' pan-KRAS approach (RMC-6236) casts a shadow — the field is converging on pan-RAS, not allele-specific, as the end state.

04

TME Is the New Battleground

1,672 abstracts address the tumor microenvironment — nearly half of all presentations. The field has accepted that tumor-cell-only targeting is insufficient. Stromal reprogramming, myeloid modulation, and spatial biology (165 abstracts) are the frontier methods.

Emerging signals →
05

Degraders Find Their Footing

72 PROTAC/degrader abstracts — a small but rapidly maturing category. KRAS (13 mentions) and BRD4 (6) are the dominant degrader targets. The DAC (Degrader Antibody Conjugate) format, combining targeted delivery with protein degradation, is the most notable structural innovation.

Modality breakdown →
06

Solid Tumors Find New Surface Antigens

The limiting factor for ADCs, bispecifics, and CAR-T in solid tumors has always been target quality. AACR 2026 shows the field is finding answers: B7-H3 (91 mentions, pan-solid-tumor expression), CDH17 (44 mentions, GI-specific), PSMA (110 mentions, expanding beyond prostate), and DLL3 (72 mentions, SCLC + neuroendocrine). These are the targets that could unlock solid tumor cell therapy and ADC expansion over the next five years.
