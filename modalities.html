Overview

The Modality Stack

Modality frequency at AACR reflects where early-stage R&D capital is flowing two to five years before it shows up at ASCO. Targeted modalities dominate, and the combination of modality versatility with precision targeting is the defining theme of AACR 2026.

Cell therapy abstracts (1,581 by broad definition) are inflated by T-cell biology research that is not manufactured cell product development. The more precise counts: CAR-T (122), TIL therapy (~40), NK cell (~85), TCR-T (~10).

Bispecifics (407) overtook ADC (267) in abstract volume — reflecting that the bispecific format has a longer history and more clinical programs — but ADC is growing faster year-over-year and has converted more clinical wins at ASCO recently. Both are accelerating.

Format convergence: ADCs, bispecifics, and CAR-T are all converging on the same emerging surface antigens. B7-H3, DLL3, and CLDN18.2 each appear in multiple modality contexts at AACR 2026. The target is the bottleneck, not the format.

ADC

Target Diversification in Full Force

267 ADC-focused abstracts, with the target landscape diversifying rapidly. HER2 still leads (59 co-mentions within ADC abstracts), but the competitive question in HER2 is no longer whether to use an ADC — it is which ADC, which epitope, and which payload. Biparatopic HER2 programs (JSKN003, TQB2102 — ASCO 2024/2025 data) are built specifically to address T-DXd resistance.

TROP2 (32) is the clear second target. Sacituzumab govitecan established the format. Datopotamab deruxtecan (Dato-DXd) is building a franchise across NSCLC, breast, and endometrial. The DXd payload plus TROP2 targeting is working in multiple tumors.

B7-H3 (11) and DLL3 (7) are notable for pace. Both went from zero ADC abstracts three years ago to meaningful AACR 2026 representation. CDH17 (7) is the most interesting new GI target in ADC format and has no late-stage clinical competition yet.

The most structurally novel format at AACR 2026 is the DAC — Degrader Antibody Conjugate. ADC-2419 conjugates an IKZF1/3 degrader to a CD38 antibody in myeloma, using antibody targeting to deliver an intracellular PROTAC payload. This solves the cell permeability barrier that limits PROTAC clinical utility and is applicable to any intracellular target with a surface-antigen-linked delivery option.

Bispecific Antibodies

The Fastest-Growing Advanced Modality

407 abstracts reference bispecific antibodies — the largest advanced modality at AACR 2026. Heme oncology programs (BCMA, CD20, CD3) are the most clinically mature. Solid tumor programs are the scientific frontier.

DLL3×CD3 in SCLC, EGFR×HER2/HER3 in NSCLC and breast, and CLDN18.2×CD3 in gastric are the three clearest solid tumor bispecific theses at AACR 2026. Each pairs a tumor-selective antigen with T-cell engagement.

The amivantamab framework — EGFR×MET co-engagement, not immune engagement — validated a different bispecific logic in NSCLC: blocking two growth factor receptor pathways simultaneously rather than engaging immune cells. MARIPOSA (ESMO/ASCO 2024) data built on this. The co-engagement bispecific format is now being applied to other receptor pairs in multiple tumors.

The bispecific ADC format (izalogamab-brenartunimab, EGFR×HER3 plus DXd payload) showed 85.7% confirmed PRs in EGFR ex20ins NSCLC Phase 1. This is the logical convergence of two modalities: multi-target engagement and cytotoxic payload delivery in one molecule. The ex20ins NSCLC data specifically is outstanding given the historically poor outcomes in that patient population.

CAR-T and Cell Therapy

First Real Signals in Solid Tumors

122 CAR-T abstracts. The most important pattern: solid tumor programs are appearing in growing numbers, a sharp shift from two years ago when heme oncology dominated entirely.

GBM (B7-H3, EGFRvIII). Intracranial CAR-T delivery partially solves the trafficking and BBB problem. CARv3-TEAM-E uses EGFRvIII CAR-T with bystander killing to address antigen heterogeneity — cells that have lost EGFRvIII expression still get killed by the TEAM component. B7-H3 CAR-T programs show activity with the same intracranial delivery rationale.

SCLC (DLL3). First solid tumor-selective antigen with sufficient expression density to drive clinical CAR-T activity. AFNT-211 and LB2102 both showed early Phase 1 signals at 2025-26 meetings.

HCC (GPC3). Liver tropism of systemically administered CAR-T may be an advantage rather than a liability in hepatocellular carcinoma. Multiple GPC3 CAR-T programs active in early development.

CEA programs in CRC and NSCLC show early clinical activity. Persistence remains the key metric, since CAR-T durability in solid tumors has been the limiting factor.
