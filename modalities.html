The Modality Stack

Modality frequency at AACR reflects where early-stage R&D capital is flowing — 2–5 years before it shows up at ASCO. The pattern is clear: targeted modalities are dominating, and the combination of modality versatility (ADC, bispecific) with precision targeting (novel surface antigens) is the defining theme of AACR 2026.


Cell therapy abstracts (1,581) are inflated by the broad definition — the category captures T cell biology research, not just manufactured cell products. The more focused counts: CAR-T (122), TIL therapy (~40), NK cell (~30), TCR-T (~25).

The structural trend: ADCs (267), Bispecifics (431), and CAR-T (122) are all growing simultaneously in solid tumors — and all three are converging on the same new surface antigens (B7-H3, DLL3, CLDN18.2). The target is the bottleneck, not the modality format.

ADC Deep Dive

The ADC Wave: Target Diversification in Full Force

271 abstracts focus on ADC programs — and the target landscape within ADC research is rapidly diversifying beyond the established triumvirate of HER2, TROP2, and Nectin-4. AACR 2026 shows a field actively solving the "next target" problem for ADCs.


HER2 still leads (59 co-mentions within ADC abstracts), but the HER2 ADC space is maturing — the frontier is in biparatopic HER2 ADCs that overcome trastuzumab-resistant epitopes and T-DXd resistance mechanisms.


TROP2 (33) is the clear second. Sacituzumab govitecan established the format; datopotamab deruxtecan (Dato-DXd) is building a franchise across NSCLC, breast, and endometrial. The DXd payload + TROP2 targeting appears to be a winning combination in multiple tumors.


B7-H3 (11) and DLL3 (7) are notable for their pace of program creation — from zero ADC abstracts three years ago to meaningful representation now. CDH17 (7) is the most interesting new GI target in ADC format.

Novel format signal: The DAC (Degrader Antibody Conjugate) — combining targeted antibody delivery with intracellular PROTAC payload — appeared at AACR 2026 in multiple myeloma. IKZF1/3 degrader conjugated to CD38 antibody (ADC-2419). The concept of using ADC targeting to solve PROTAC cell permeability problems is structurally interesting for targets previously inaccessible to degraders.

Bispecific Antibodies

Bispecifics: The Fastest-Growing Modality

431 bispecific abstracts make this the most represented advanced modality at AACR 2026 — consistent with the ASCO trend showing 9.9× growth over the past decade. The heme oncology programs (BCMA, CD20, CD3) are most clinically mature; the solid tumor programs are where the scientific frontier sits.


The solid tumor bispecific story is being written around DLL3 × CD3 in SCLC, EGFR × HER2/HER3 in NSCLC/breast, and CLDN18.2 × CD3 in gastric/GEJ. Each combines a tumor-selective antigen with T-cell engagement.


The amivantamab (EGFR × MET) framework — two growth factor receptors on the same tumor cell — validated the bispecific approach in NSCLC with MARIPOSA data. This "co-engagement" format (not immune engagement) is now being applied to other receptor pairs.

Format convergence: The combination of ADC + bispecific formats is appearing at AACR 2026 in the "bispecific ADC" (e.g., izalogamab-brenartunimab, EGFR×HER3 + DXd payload). This is the logical endpoint — multi-target engagement with cytotoxic payload delivery. Early efficacy data is remarkable: 85.7% confirmed PRs in EGFR ex20ins NSCLC.

CAR-T in Solid Tumors: First Real Signals

122 CAR-T abstracts represent a meaningful cohort, and the most important pattern is that solid tumor CAR-T programs are appearing in numbers — a sharp contrast to two years ago when heme oncology dominated entirely.


The solid tumor CAR-T programs at AACR 2026 cluster around four strategies:


1. GBM (B7-H3 + EGFRvIII): Intracranial CAR-T infusion directly bypasses systemic trafficking limitations. B7-H3 and EGFRvIII CAR-T programs are showing signals in GBM — perhaps the only solid tumor where the delivery problem is partially solved by local administration.


2. SCLC (DLL3): DLL3 is the first solid tumor-selective antigen with enough expression density to drive CAR-T. Multiple programs (AFNT-211, LB2102) showed early Phase 1 signals at ASCO/AACR 2025–26.


3. GI tumors (GPC3, CLDN18.2): HCC (GPC3) and gastric/pancreatic (CLDN18.2) programs in early development. Liver tropism of CAR-T may be an advantage for HCC.


4. Armored/logic-gated CAR-T: Programs that co-express cytokines (IL-2, IL-15), safety switches, or use logic gating (AND/NOT gates) to address on-target off-tumor toxicity. This is the engineering frontier, primarily academic at AACR 2026.

Investment question: The solid tumor CAR-T programs generating the most AACR abstracts are still Phase 1 with small cohorts. The timing question is whether the 76% YoY growth in solid tumor CAR-T abstracts seen at ASCO 2024 continues to translate into clinical signals, or whether the engineering challenges (trafficking, persistence, TME suppression) continue to limit efficacy.
