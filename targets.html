Emerging Surface Antigens

The Solid Tumor Target Frontier

The persistent limit on ADC, bispecific, and CAR-T programs in solid tumors is target quality: high tumor expression, low normal tissue expression, and accessible epitopes. AACR 2026 shows the field making real progress beyond HER2 and TROP2.

B7-H3
35 abstracts
Pan-solid-tumor expression with limited normal tissue. Multiple modalities — ADC, CAR-T, bispecific — are all targeting B7-H3 simultaneously. The strongest signals are in GBM (intracranial delivery), SCLC, prostate, and lung. This is beginning to look like what CD19 looked like in lymphoma before the cell therapy wave.
HIGH PRIORITY
PSMA
20 abstracts
PLUVICTO validated the radioligand format in prostate. The research at AACR 2026 is asking two new questions: what are the ADC and bispecific PSMA programs, and does PSMA expression in tumor neovasculature (not just prostate epithelium) enable solid tumor expansion more broadly.
VALIDATED
DLL3
23 abstracts
Defined the SCLC field. Tarlatamab validated the target. ZL-1310 ADC (68% ORR, 80% in brain mets) pushed the data forward. CAR-T programs are entering early clinical stage. The neuroendocrine expression pattern may extend to large-cell neuroendocrine and extrapulmonary SCLC.
RISING FAST
CLDN18.2
15 abstracts
Gastric and pancreatic anchor target. Zolbetuximab provided clinical proof-of-concept. ADC programs (ASKB589, IBI343) and CAR-T programs are now in early development in CLDN18.2-high populations. Present in roughly 40% of PDAC, making it one of the few tractable surface antigens in pancreatic cancer.
RISING
CDH17
10 abstracts
GI-specific cadherin with high expression in colorectal, gastric, and pancreatic tumors and very limited normal tissue expression outside the GI tract. Still early — no Phase 2 data in the AACR 2026 set — but the preclinical rationale from multiple academic and biotech programs is converging on this target as the next GI ADC anchor.
EARLY FRONTIER
HER3
16 abstracts
Near-universal expression in NSCLC, now gaining momentum as an ADC target post-osimertinib resistance. Patritumab deruxtecan addresses the pan-ErbB bypass mechanism that drives resistance in EGFRm disease. The NSCLC post-osi population is a well-defined, large, and currently underserved group.

KRAS Ecosystem

KRAS: Three Distinct Fronts

155 gene-extraction-list mentions of KRAS — and 170 per case-sensitive text search. The ecosystem has three distinct and largely non-overlapping fronts, each with its own competitive dynamics.

Allele-specific inhibitors (G12C, G12D, G12V). Sotorasib and adagrasib validated G12C. G12D programs — MRTX113, GFH375, TSN16 — are now the leading edge, reflecting the much larger clinical opportunity. G12D is the most prevalent KRAS allele in PDAC (roughly 40% of KRAS-mutant tumors) and about 36% of KRAS-mutant NSCLC. G12V programs are less advanced.

Pan-RAS inhibitors. Revolution Medicines' daraxonrasib (RMC-6236) is in Phase 3 (RASolute 302). The pan-RAS approach covers all KRAS mutations plus NRAS and HRAS, which matters in tumors with mixed mutation prevalence. Success in RASolute 302 would likely disintermediate allele-specific programs in most indications.

KRAS degraders. 13 abstracts combine KRAS inhibition with PROTAC or molecular degradation approaches. The hypothesis is that degradation eliminates the feedback reactivation mechanism that limits catalytic inhibition. Early but structurally different from the other two fronts.

Venture read: G12C-only KRAS programs are increasingly crowded and face pan-RAS disintermediation. The more interesting bets are G12D-selective programs before the pan-RAS data readout, KRAS degraders as a resistance solution, and KRAS combination strategies (plus IO, plus SOS1) that pan-RAS inhibitors do not automatically provide.

DNA Damage Response

Expanding the Synthetic Lethality Map

267 abstracts address DNA damage response pathways. PARP inhibitors opened the synthetic lethality category. The field is now building toward a full DDR toolkit: ATR, WEE1, ATM, CHK1/2, and DNA-PK in various combination settings.

WEE1 in ARID1A-mutant tumors is the most active synthetic lethality hypothesis at AACR 2026. ARID1A is a chromatin remodeling gene mutated in roughly 50% of ovarian clear cell carcinoma and 20-35% of gastric and endometrial cancers. WEE1 inhibition (adavosertib-successor programs) in ARID1A-deficient cells shows preclinical selectivity that multiple groups are now testing clinically.

ATR as a drug target appears in 26 abstracts (word-boundary search). The clinical interest in ATR inhibition as a PARP inhibitor resistance mechanism is building. ATRi plus PARPi combinations are in multiple Phase 1/2 studies.

PRMT5 inhibitors (11 abstracts) exploit synthetic lethality in MTAP-deleted tumors. MTAP deletion occurs in roughly 15% of all cancers and creates a metabolic vulnerability that PRMT5 inhibitors exploit selectively. Multiple Phase 1/2 programs are active.
