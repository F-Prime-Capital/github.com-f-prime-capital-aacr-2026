Emerging Surface Antigens

The New Target Frontier for Solid Tumors

The long-standing problem with solid tumor cell therapy and ADC programs has been target quality — high tumor expression, low normal tissue expression, and accessible epitopes. AACR 2026 shows the field is finally finding answers beyond HER2 and TROP2.

B7-H3
91 abstract mentions
Pan-solid-tumor expression with low normal tissue. Emerging as the CD19 of solid tumors — a target that could anchor multiple modalities: ADC, CAR-T, bispecific. Strong activity in GBM, SCLC, prostate, and lung.
HIGH PRIORITY
PSMA
110 abstract mentions
The radioligand benchmark — PLUVICTO validated the category. Now expanding into ADC and bispecific modalities, and earlier disease settings. Non-prostate PSMA expression in solid tumor neovasculature is an underexplored angle.
HIGH PRIORITY
DLL3
72 abstract mentions
The defining SCLC surface antigen. Tarlatamab (BiTE) validated the target; ZL-1310 ADC showed 68% ORR including brain mets. The neuroendocrine expression pattern extends to large-cell neuroendocrine and some GI tumors.
RISING
CLDN18.2
67 abstract mentions
Gastric/GEJ and pancreatic anchor target. Zolbetuximab validated clinical proof-of-concept. Now the ADC and CAR-T race begins — ASKB589, IBI343, and multiple Chinese programs building fast.
RISING
CDH17
44 abstract mentions
GI-specific cadherin — high expression in colorectal, gastric, and pancreatic tumors. Very limited normal tissue. Emerging as the most promising new GI ADC target. Early data from multiple academic and biotech programs.
RISING
HER3
58 abstract mentions
Long overlooked but now gaining momentum as an ADC target. Patritumab deruxtecan (HER3-DXd) showed activity in EGFR-mutant NSCLC post-osimertinib — a critical unmet need. HER3 expression is near-universal in NSCLC.
RISING
GPC3
33 abstract mentions
HCC-selective glypican. Multiple CAR-T and ADC programs in early development. The HCC indication is underserved for cell therapy given liver tropism challenges — GPC3 specificity is the key advantage.
WATCH
Mesothelin
24 abstract mentions
Mesothelioma, ovarian, and pancreatic expression. CAR-T programs have faced on-target off-tumor toxicity challenges. New armored CAR-T and ADC approaches with better therapeutic windows are in development.
WATCH

Folate receptor alpha — mirvetuximab soravtansine validated in platinum-resistant ovarian. ADC programs expanding to endometrial and lung adenocarcinoma. IHC scoring harmonization remains a challenge for patient selection.

The KRAS Ecosystem: From Undruggable to Contested

KRAS is the most mentioned oncogene in the dataset at 155 mentions — nearly tied with HER2. But unlike HER2, KRAS is a problem being actively solved rather than a solved problem. The ecosystem has three distinct fronts:


Allele-specific (G12C, G12D, G12V): Sotorasib and adagrasib validated G12C. G12D programs (MRTX113, GFH375, TSN16) are now the leading edge. G12D is ~36% of KRAS-mutant NSCLC and ~90% of KRAS-mutant pancreatic — the bigger clinical opportunity.


Pan-RAS/pan-KRAS: Revolution Medicines' daraxonrasib (RMC-6236) is the most credible pan-KRAS program, now in Phase 3 (RASolute 302). If successful, it obsoletes allele-specific approaches in all but the most targeted settings.


KRAS degraders: 13 abstracts combine KRAS with PROTAC/molecular glue approaches. Early but structurally interesting — degradation may overcome the resistance mechanisms (feedback reactivation) that plague catalytic inhibition.

VC thesis: The bet on G12C-only programs is increasingly crowded and may be disintermediated by pan-RAS. The more interesting venture question is KRAS combination strategies — IO + KRAS inhibitor (KRYSTAL-7 data), and KRAS + SOS1 for feedback resistance.

1,431 abstracts mention DNA damage response pathways — second only to TME in theme frequency. PARP inhibitors opened the category; the field is now building toward a full DDR inhibitor toolkit targeting ATR, WEE1, ATM, CHK1/2, and DNA-PK in combination.
