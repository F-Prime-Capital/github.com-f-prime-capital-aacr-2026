From Lab to Clinic

Stage distribution, modality breakdown, and named programs across the 357 clinical-stage abstracts at AACR 2026. AACR is overwhelmingly preclinical — which makes the clinical programs here a signal of what has already survived the first filter.

The Funnel

3,489 Abstracts, 357 Clinical

96% of AACR 2026 is preclinical. That is not a weakness — it is the point. AACR is where programs are at the stage where venture can still shape outcomes. The 357 clinical-stage abstracts (sum of Phase 1, 2, and 3 counts; some abstracts span multiple phases) represent programs that have cleared IND and generated human data good enough to present at a major conference. They deserve closer attention than their minority share suggests.

The funnel compression is steep: 3,347 preclinical, 245 Phase 1, 82 Phase 2, 30 Phase 3. Each jump is roughly a 3-4x reduction. The modality mix changes significantly across stages — IO dominates Phase 3 (legacy of the checkpoint era), while ADC and bispecific are concentrated in Phase 1, reflecting when those programs entered clinical development.

45% of Phase 1 programs at AACR 2026 have explicit biomarker selection criteria — a meaningful increase from the IO era, when PD-L1 IHC was often the only selection tool. 43% are combination programs.

Patterns across these programs: Three structural themes appear repeatedly. First, synthetic lethality is being applied to new paralog pairs beyond BRCA/PARP — SMARCA4/SMARCA2 and MTAP/PRMT5 are the leading examples. Second, novel surface antigens for ADC delivery continue to emerge — CDH17, ADAM9, 5T4, and CLDN18.2 are each in first-in-human ADC programs. Third, combination modalities are collapsing — the mRNA-encoded trispecific and the ML-designed bispecific both represent formats that would not have been manufacturable at clinical scale three years ago.

What AACR Preclinical Predicts

The Translation Timeline

The lag from AACR preclinical presentation to ASCO Phase 1 data is typically two to four years. Target validation appearing at AACR 2026 in volume — CDH17, ADAM9, 5T4, PRMT5/MTAP — represents programs that will generate IND filings in 2026–2027 and first-in-human data at ASCO or ESMO in 2028–2029.

The preclinical programs with the highest translation probability share three characteristics: a clean surface antigen with strong IHC data across multiple tumor types, a validated payload or modality matched to the biology, and a resistance mechanism rationale for why this target matters beyond the existing standard of care.

CAR-T has zero Phase 3 representation at AACR 2026. Solid tumor CAR-T programs are at Phase 1, heme programs are at Phase 1/2. The Phase 3 window for solid tumor CAR-T, if the current clinical signals hold, is likely 2028–2030 at the earliest.
