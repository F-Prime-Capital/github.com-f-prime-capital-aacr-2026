Volume Analysis

Research Volume by Indication

Breast cancer (568) leads all solid tumor indications at AACR 2026, reflecting the breadth of the target landscape — HER2, TROP2, ER, BRCA, CDK4/6, and PI3K/AKT all driving active research. The indication is also one of the few where multiple modalities have clinically validated data, enabling combination and sequencing research.


Colorectal (451), NSCLC (362), SCLC (353), and Pancreatic (250) round out the top five solid tumor indications. Notably, SCLC's volume (353) is disproportionately high relative to its incidence — reflecting the DLL3/tarlatamab inflection that has opened the indication after two decades of stagnation.


Prostate (197), GBM (195), Ovarian (166), and Melanoma (160) maintain consistent research investment. GBM's volume is striking given the lack of approved targeted therapies — the field keeps trying.

The underserved gap: Thyroid (8 abstracts), Cervical (35), and Endometrial (65) are underrepresented relative to their incidence and mortality burden. Endometrial cancer in particular — the most common gynecologic malignancy in the US — is receiving less research attention than its clinical need warrants, despite the fact that ER+ endometrial is a growing indication for CDK4/6 and ER-targeting agents.

Indication Deep Dives

Where the Science Is Moving

SCLC
353 abstracts · High momentum
SCLC went from a deadzone to one of the most active research areas in two years, powered by DLL3 and the tarlatamab (BiTE) validation. AACR 2026 shows the DLL3 modality race in full force: ADC (ZL-1310, 68% ORR), bispecific (tarlatamab), CAR-T (AFNT-211, LB2102), and trispecific (ZG006, DLL3/CD3/DLL3). IMforte's lurbinectedin data at ASCO 2025 added a maintenance layer. The field has gone from no actionable targets to three modalities converging on DLL3 in under three years.
ACCELERATING
Pancreatic
250 abstracts · Cracking but not broken
After 14 years of flatlined median OS at 11.1 months, AACR 2026 shows the first credible wave of new biology. KRAS G12D programs (dominant PDAC allele at ~90%) are the primary hope. CLDN18.2 ADC programs (IBI343, ASKB589) show early ORR signals in CLDN18.2-high patients (~40% of PDAC). CDH17 is emerging as a GI-specific ADC target with strong preclinical rationale. Revolution Medicines' pan-KRAS program is in Phase 3. The mood has shifted from "hopeless" to "possible."
FIRST CRACKS
Breast Cancer
568 abstracts · Maturing ecosystem
The most research-active solid tumor at AACR 2026. The field has subdivided into HER2-high (T-DXd dominated), HER2-low (T-DXd vs SG vs DATO-DXd), TNBC (SG, immunotherapy), and ER+/HER2- (CDK4/6 + next-gen ER approaches). The biparatopic HER2 ADC programs appearing at AACR 2026 (JSKN003, IBI354, TQB2102) are targeting T-DXd resistance. ESR1 mutant BRCA is a significant combinatorial target in ER+ late-line disease.
MATURE ECOSYSTEM
GBM
195 abstracts · Engineering-driven
GBM remains one of the hardest problems in oncology — the Stupp protocol has been unchanged for 20 years. AACR 2026 shows two approaches gaining traction: local/intracranial delivery of CAR-T (bypassing the BBB and trafficking problem) and B7-H3-targeting agents (pan-solid-tumor expression including GBM). EGFRvIII CAR-T with bystander killing mechanism (CARv3-TEAM-E) is the most mechanistically elegant program. No Phase 3 catalysts yet, but the early clinical signals are more encouraging than prior generations.
ENGINEERING FRONTIER
CRC
451 abstracts · Crowded, complex
Colorectal is heavily researched but progress is stratified by molecular subtype. MSI-H/dMMR (~15%) is solved with IO. KRAS-mutant (~45%) has KRAS inhibitor programs. BRAF V600E (~10%) has BRAF/MEK combinations. The major unmet need is MSS/proficient MMR (most patients), who don't respond to IO and lack targeted options. CDH17 as a GI-specific ADC target could be transformative here. The KRAS + IO combination data from KRYSTAL-7 is the most important CRC-relevant finding at AACR 2026.
STRATIFIED
Prostate
197 abstracts · Radiopharm frontier
PSMA-targeting (110 mentions total) is the dominant AACR 2026 signal in prostate. PLUVICTO validated radioligand therapy; now the field is asking: what comes after PSMA? PSMA-targeting ADC and bispecific programs are active. Beyond PSMA, BRCA/HRR-selected patients receiving PARP inhibitors (olaparib, rucaparib, niraparib) are a maturing subgroup. The PSMA expression in tumor neovasculature outside prostate is an underexplored expansion opportunity — multiple programs exploring this at AACR.

AACR is fundamentally an academic conference — 65% of abstracts originate from universities, research institutes, and hospitals. This is a feature, not a bug: AACR is where the next five years of industry programs are being discovered.


The top industry presenters by abstract count are AstraZeneca (46), Roche/Genentech (41), and Revolution Medicines (25). AZ's representation reflects their ADC + KRAS + DDR franchise depth. Revolution Medicines' outsized presence (25 abstracts) relative to their size is a signal of platform breadth.


The academic → industry translation pattern: targets and mechanisms discovered at AACR in Year 0 tend to appear in biotech Series A filings in Year 1–2, and ASCO Phase 1 data in Year 3–4. AACR is the earliest point in that funnel where systematic tracking adds value.
