Why This Matters

Patient Selection Is the Other Half of the Drug

Every practice-changing targeted therapy of the past decade has depended on a companion diagnostic or biomarker enrichment strategy. KEYNOTE-024 worked because of PD-L1 TPS 50%+ selection. PARP inhibitors work in HRD-selected patients. T-DXd in HER2-low created an entirely new IHC scoring category. The drug and the diagnostic are inseparable — and AACR 2026 is where the next generation of both are being developed simultaneously.

Biomarker research at AACR 2026 spans a spectrum from fundamental discovery (single-cell sequencing to understand tumor heterogeneity) through translational tools (spatial biology to map the TME) to clinical instruments (ctDNA for early detection and MRD monitoring). Each layer feeds the next.

The volume is substantial. 300 single-cell sequencing abstracts. 195 ctDNA and liquid biopsy abstracts. 161 spatial transcriptomics abstracts. 131 proteomics abstracts. 109 companion diagnostic or biomarker-selected program abstracts. 62 AI/digital pathology abstracts. These are not incidental mentions — they represent dedicated research programs building the infrastructure on which the next decade of oncology will run.

Investment framing: Diagnostics and biomarker companies are often undervalued relative to drug companies, but their commercial leverage is high — every approved targeted therapy requires a companion diagnostic or patient selection tool, and those tools travel with the drug for its entire commercial life. Layer 1

Liquid Biopsy and ctDNA

195 abstracts address ctDNA, liquid biopsy, or circulating tumor DNA. The applications span early detection, treatment selection, MRD monitoring, and resistance mechanism identification — four distinct use cases that represent different market opportunities.

Early detection is the largest commercial opportunity but the hardest biology. AACR 2026 abstracts in this space are working on sensitivity at early stage (where tumor DNA shedding is low) and specificity (distinguishing clonal hematopoiesis from tumor-derived signal). Guardant appears in 21 abstracts, reflecting its clinical deployment for treatment selection and MRD monitoring across multiple tumor types.

MRD monitoring post-surgery and post-treatment is the most mature near-term application. ctDNA clearance as a surrogate endpoint for adjuvant therapy benefit is being tested in multiple Phase 2/3 trials referenced in AACR 2026 abstracts. If validated, it would shorten adjuvant trial timelines substantially.

Resistance mechanism identification via serial ctDNA is becoming standard in academic centers for programs where resistance is the key scientific question — EGFRm NSCLC post-osimertinib, ER+ breast post-CDK4/6, and KRAS-mutant CRC are the three most active use cases in the AACR dataset.

Tempus (18 mentions) and NanoString (20 mentions) are also prominently represented, reflecting their role in multi-omic profiling and spatial biology infrastructure respectively.

Layer 2

Spatial Biology and the TME Map

161 spatial transcriptomics abstracts at AACR 2026. The technology has matured from proof-of-concept to a standard tool in TME characterization — and is now generating data that will underpin the next generation of IO patient selection biomarkers.

The core scientific question is: what does the spatial organization of immune cells within a tumor predict about drug response? AACR 2026 abstracts are establishing that immune exclusion patterns — where T cells are present at the tumor margin but not inside — are associated with IO resistance in ways that bulk RNA-seq fails to capture. Spatially resolved data identifies this pattern directly.

10x Genomics (Xenium), Vizgen (MERFISH), and Bruker (CODEX) are the primary platforms appearing in AACR 2026 abstracts. 10x Genomics appears in 22 abstracts — a significant presence for a tool company. NanoString (20 mentions) reflects GeoMx and CosMx spatial profiling use. The infrastructure layer is being established at academic centers now, which means spatial data will be routinely generated alongside clinical trial biopsies within two to three years.

The investable question is: which companies are building the AI interpretation layer on top of this spatial data? Raw spatial images require sophisticated analysis to extract actionable biomarkers. The 62 AI/digital pathology abstracts at AACR 2026 represent early versions of this analytical layer.

Layer 3

Single-Cell Sequencing and the Heterogeneity Problem

300 single-cell sequencing abstracts — the largest individual technology tool category in AACR 2026. The primary scientific use is tumor heterogeneity characterization and immune cell state mapping, both of which feed directly into patient selection problems.

The top targets appearing in single-cell abstracts are PD-1 (26), EGFR (14), PD-L1 (14), CD8 (13), and CD4 (11) — reflecting that single-cell is predominantly used to understand the immune compartment and its relationship to tumor antigen expression. CAF subtypes (appearing as "CAFs" in 6 abstracts) and macrophage polarization states are active research themes.

The translation question for single-cell is standardization. Academic centers are generating single-cell data from patient biopsies at AACR 2026, but the analytical pipelines, reference atlases, and clinical interpretation frameworks are not yet standardized enough for use as regulatory-grade biomarkers. The companies building that standardization layer — reference databases, clinical-grade scRNA pipelines, regulatory-compliant assay platforms — are the investable opportunity in this category.

Organoids and PDX models appear in 344 abstracts, largely as drug testing platforms rather than patient selection tools. However, organoid drug response profiling as a predictive biomarker (matching tumor organoids to patient drug response) is an active research direction at AACR 2026 that bridges the two categories.

Layer 4

The Synthetic Lethality Biomarker Stack

The biomarkers enabling synthetic lethality programs represent a distinct and growing category. Unlike PD-L1 (a continuous expression marker with fuzzy cutoffs), synthetic lethality biomarkers are binary loss-of-function events that define the patient population with high precision.

BRCA1/2
51/35 as primary target (gene extraction / text search)
The original synthetic lethality biomarker. Germline and somatic BRCA1/2 loss defines the HRD population for PARP inhibitors in breast, ovarian, prostate, and pancreatic cancers. Well-validated, commercially deployed (Foundation Medicine, Myriad). The question now is which additional DDR genes beyond BRCA expand the biomarker-selected population.
ESTABLISHED
MTAP deletion
12 mentions
MTAP deletion occurs in ~15% of all cancers and creates metabolic vulnerability to PRMT5 inhibition. Binary, detectable by standard NGS. Multiple PRMT5 inhibitor programs (including BMS-986504 in GBM, the AACR 2026 FIH program) are selecting patients on MTAP loss. The biomarker is well-characterized but the drug class is still in Phase 1.
EMERGING
SMARCA4 loss
14 mentions
SMARCA4 deficiency creates dependency on its paralog SMARCA2. PLX-61639 (at AACR 2026 in Phase 1) exploits this with a SMARCA2 degrader. SMARCA4 loss occurs in ~10% of NSCLC, in the rare small-cell carcinoma of the ovary hypercalcemic type (SCCOHT), and other solid tumors. IHC and NGS detection are both available.
EMERGING
ARID1A mutation
22 mentions
ARID1A loss creates WEE1 dependency in ovarian, endometrial, and gastric tumors. The WEE1-ARID1A synthetic lethality hypothesis is the most active non-BRCA synthetic lethality story at AACR 2026. Detection is straightforward by NGS. The challenge is that no WEE1 inhibitor has been approved yet — the biomarker is ahead of the drug.
WATCH
HRD (beyond BRCA)
37 abstracts
Homologous recombination deficiency scores (HRD score, genomic instability score) expand the PARP inhibitor-eligible population beyond BRCA mutation carriers. Programs using HRD as a companion diagnostic are active in ovarian, breast, and pancreatic cancer. Myriad myChoice CDx and Foundation Medicine CDx are the commercial assays.
MATURING
MSI / MMR status
61 mentions
MSI-H/dMMR is the original tumor-agnostic IO biomarker (pembrolizumab approval, KEYNOTE-158). The AACR 2026 abstracts referencing MSI status are mostly using it as a stratification variable or studying the biology of MMR deficiency rather than developing new drugs around it. A mature, commercially deployed biomarker.

Venture Angle

Where the Diagnostics Investment Goes

The diagnostics investment thesis at AACR 2026 is in two places. The first is the AI/digital pathology layer — 64 abstracts — where companies are building computational tools that extract biomarker signals from existing tissue specimens without new assay development. Whole-slide image analysis for spatial immune patterns, H&E-derived biomarker prediction, and pathology foundation models are all active research directions. The commercial advantage is that no new clinical assay is required — the interpretation runs on existing pathology infrastructure.

The second is the spatial biology standardization layer. The spatial platforms (10x, NanoString, Bruker/Akoya) are largely built. The gap is analytical: converting spatial images to reproducible, regulatory-grade biomarker calls. Companies building standardized analytical pipelines for spatial TME characterization, with reference datasets and clinical validation, are building infrastructure that every immune oncology program will need within five years.
