Signal #1

AI Has Become the Default Research Method

2,277 abstracts mention AI or machine learning — that is 65% of all AACR 2026 presentations. Three years ago this number was in the single digits. The adoption curve has been vertical.


The applications are not concentrated in any single use case. AI appears across: spatial transcriptomics analysis, histopathology image classification, multi-omic target identification, drug-drug interaction prediction, patient stratification, single-cell data processing, and structure-based drug design.


The investment implication is counterintuitive: this ubiquity means AI as a standalone platform thesis is weakening. "We use AI" is no longer differentiating. The question is which specific AI capability creates a defensible, reproducible discovery advantage — and whether that advantage manifests in the quality of targets, clinical stage velocity, or both.


The 234 abstracts specifically focused on AI in drug discovery (not just analysis) are the higher-signal subset. Programs using foundation models trained on protein structure + multi-omic data to generate novel target hypotheses represent a structurally different approach from those using ML for patient classification.

VC lens: GSK's "in silico triage pipeline for multi-omic evaluation of surface protein targets" (presented at AACR 2026 in ADC target context) is a signal that large pharma is systematizing AI-driven target identification. The biotech opportunity is in the companies doing this faster, with more novel training data, or in more defensible target classes.

Signal #2

The Tumor Microenvironment: Still Frontier, Now Tractable

1,672 abstracts address the tumor microenvironment — making it the second most-discussed theme after AI. The scientific consensus has shifted: the TME is not just a barrier to therapy, it is an active target space.


The four most active TME research fronts at AACR 2026:


Myeloid cell reprogramming: Tumor-associated macrophages (TAMs), MDSCs, and neutrophils are the dominant immunosuppressive TME populations in solid tumors. Programs targeting CSF1R, CCR2, SIRPα/CD47, and IL-6/STAT3 signaling are attempting to reprogram the myeloid compartment from immunosuppressive to inflammatory.


Cancer-associated fibroblasts (CAFs): The stroma is the physical barrier to ADC and cell therapy penetration in pancreatic, ovarian, and CRC. FAP-targeting (fibroblast activation protein) bispecifics and ADCs are emerging as a category to convert CAFs from barriers to drug delivery vehicles.


Spatial biology (165 abstracts): Technologies like Visium, Xenium, CODEX, and MERFISH are enabling high-resolution mapping of TME composition and spatial organization. The research at AACR is starting to identify "immune desert" vs "excluded" vs "inflamed" TME patterns with predictive biomarker value for IO response.


Metabolic reprogramming (405 abstracts): TME acidity, glucose competition, and adenosine pathway suppression are mechanistic links between tumor metabolism and immune evasion. STING agonists (32 target mentions), IDO pathway revisited with better patient selection, and adenosine receptor antagonists are the most active programs.

Signal #3

580 "First-in-Class" or "Novel" Programs

580 abstracts contain language describing novel mechanisms, first-in-class approaches, or first-in-human data. These are the highest-signal programs for venture scouts. The categories below represent the most structurally interesting clusters.

DAC Format
Novel format
Degrader Antibody Conjugate — uses antibody targeting to deliver intracellular PROTAC payload. Solves the cell permeability problem for degraders. IKZF1/3 degrader + CD38 antibody in myeloma (ADC-2419) is the exemplar. Applicable to any intracellular target with adequate surface-antigen-linked tumor selectivity.
WATCH CLOSELY
Bispecific ADC
Novel format
Single molecule: bispecific antibody (two tumor targets or tumor+immune) conjugated to cytotoxic payload. Izalogamab-brenartunimab (EGFR×HER3 + DXd) showed 85.7% cPR in EGFR ex20ins NSCLC Phase 1. The format may overcome single-antigen heterogeneity and increase tumor selectivity simultaneously.
WATCH CLOSELY
Pan-RAS Degrader
Early programs
Multiple AACR 2026 programs combine KRAS inhibition with molecular degradation — aiming to eliminate the protein rather than inhibit it. Addresses the feedback reactivation resistance mechanism. G12D and G12V alleles are the primary targets since G12C covalent degradation is mechanistically different.
RISING
PRMT5 Inhibitors
73 mentions
PRMT5 (protein arginine methyltransferase 5) inhibitors represent a novel epigenetic approach with synthetic lethality in MTAP-deleted tumors (~15% of all cancers). MAT2A inhibitors partner mechanistically. Multiple Phase 1/2 programs active. The MTAP deletion biomarker provides a clean patient selection strategy.
RISING
STING Agonists
32 mentions
STING pathway activation induces innate immune responses that can convert "cold" tumors to "hot." After several failed systemic programs, the field is moving toward intratumoral injection and ADC-delivered STING agonist payloads. The combination with checkpoint blockade is the primary clinical hypothesis.
CAUTIOUS INTEREST
mRNA / LNP Programs
164 mentions
mRNA/LNP applications at AACR 2026 extend far beyond vaccines: mRNA encoding cytokines, tumor-suppressor proteins, and CAR constructs for in vivo T cell engineering. The COVID vaccine manufacturing scale-up has created a platform infrastructure that oncology programs can now access. Organ-selective LNP delivery is the key unsolved problem for solid tumors.

Signal #4

558 Resistance Mechanism Abstracts: Where the Next Programs Are Born

Resistance mechanism research is the most important lagging indicator of where the next generation of drug programs will emerge. The 558 abstracts studying acquired resistance at AACR 2026 are mapping the vulnerabilities that will become the next drug targets.


The most active resistance research categories:


T-DXd resistance in HER2+ breast: As T-DXd becomes standard of care in 2L and approaches 1L, resistance mechanisms are being mapped. Topoisomerase I mutations, MDR1 upregulation, HER2 loss/heterogeneity, and BRCA pathway reactivation are the leading mechanisms. Biparatopic HER2 ADCs at AACR 2026 are explicitly designed as T-DXd resistance solutions.


Osimertinib resistance in EGFRm NSCLC: Multiple resistance mechanisms post-osi have been characterized — C797S mutation, MET amplification, histologic transformation to SCLC, and KRAS mutation acquisition. HER3-DXd (patritumab deruxtecan) is the most advanced resistance program, addressing the pan-ErbB bypass mechanism.


CDK4/6 inhibitor resistance in ER+ breast: ESR1 mutations (ligand-binding domain) drive ~40% of resistance to CDK4/6 + AI combinations. Next-gen ER degraders (elacestrant, camizestrant, giredestrant) are now being combined with CDK4/6 and PI3K/AKT inhibitors in the resistance setting.

Pattern recognition: Every major approved drug class at AACR 2026 has a parallel resistance research program. The companies systematically mapping resistance mechanisms for drugs that will become SoC in 2–3 years are the ones building the next-wave programs. This is one of the most productive venture-relevant patterns at the conference.

Methodology

How This Analysis Was Built

This report is based on AI-processed analysis of 3,489 poster and presentation abstracts from AACR Annual Meeting 2026. The workflow:


Step 1 — PDF extraction: All downloadable posters and presentations were collected. 3,346 processed via standard extraction; 83 via OCR fallback for scanned PDFs; 54 excluded (not valid PDFs).


Step 2 — AI summarization: Each abstract was processed by a Gemma-4 model to extract: (a) structured summary with company/institution, program, mechanism, indication, modality, key data, and clinical stage; (b) list of genes and molecular targets mentioned.


Step 3 — Signal extraction: Summary and target data were analyzed programmatically for frequency, co-occurrence, and trend patterns. Modality, indication, and theme classifications use keyword matching with manual validation for top categories.


Limitations: Keyword-based classification has known false positive rates (e.g., the AML/Leukemia count is inflated by biology papers using leukemia cell lines). Academic vs. industry classification is estimated from institution name parsing. Stage classification reflects stated intent, not independent validation.
