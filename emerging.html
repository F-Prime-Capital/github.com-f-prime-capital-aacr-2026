Signal 01

AI Has Become the Default Research Method

2,277 abstracts mention machine learning or AI — 65% of all AACR 2026 presentations. Three years ago this number was in the single digits. The adoption curve has been vertical.

The applications are not concentrated in one area. AI appears in spatial transcriptomics analysis, histopathology image classification, multi-omic target identification, drug interaction prediction, patient stratification, single-cell data processing, and structure-based drug design. It is the default analysis toolkit, not a specialized capability.

The investment implication is counterintuitive: ubiquity weakens the "AI platform" thesis. "We use AI" no longer differentiates. The question is which specific AI capability produces a reproducible discovery advantage — and whether that advantage shows up in the quality of targets identified, the speed to clinical stage, or both. The 234 abstracts focused specifically on AI in drug discovery (not just analysis) are the higher-signal subset worth tracking.

GSK's presentation of an "in silico triage pipeline for multi-omic evaluation of surface protein targets" in the ADC context at AACR 2026 is a signal that large pharma is systematizing AI-driven target identification at scale. Companies doing this faster, with more defensible training data or in novel target classes, have a structural window before that gap closes.

Signal 02

TME Research Has Found Tractable Biology

1,672 abstracts address the tumor microenvironment. The scientific consensus has shifted from TME-as-barrier to TME-as-target.

Myeloid reprogramming is the most active area. Tumor-associated macrophages, MDSCs, and neutrophils are the dominant immunosuppressive populations in solid tumors. Programs targeting CSF1R, CCR2, SIRPα/CD47, and IL-6/STAT3 signaling are attempting to switch the myeloid compartment from immunosuppressive to inflammatory.

Cancer-associated fibroblasts create the physical stroma that blocks ADC and cell therapy penetration in pancreatic, ovarian, and colorectal tumors. FAP-targeting bispecifics and ADCs are emerging as a way to turn CAFs from barriers into drug delivery vehicles.

Spatial biology (165 abstracts) is providing the resolution to map TME composition meaningfully. Immune desert versus excluded versus inflamed TME patterns are being correlated with IO response in multiple tumor types. This is the precursor to TME-based patient selection biomarkers, which will matter enormously once TME-targeted drugs have Phase 2 data.

Metabolic reprogramming (405 abstracts) links tumor acidosis, glucose competition, and adenosine pathway suppression to immune evasion. STING agonists (32 mentions) and adenosine receptor antagonists are the most active programs, though both categories have faced Phase 2 disappointments that are reshaping how they are deployed — intratumoral rather than systemic, and in combination with checkpoint inhibition.

Signal 03

580 First-in-Class Programs: What's Worth Tracking

580 abstracts use language describing first-in-class mechanisms, novel approaches, or first-in-human data. The clusters below represent the most structurally significant.

DAC Format
Novel
Degrader Antibody Conjugate delivers an intracellular PROTAC payload via antibody targeting. Solves the cell permeability barrier that limits PROTAC clinical utility. IKZF1/3 degrader plus CD38 antibody in myeloma (ADC-2419) is the exemplar. Applicable to any intracellular target paired with a surface antigen for tumor selectivity.
HIGH INTEREST
Bispecific ADC
Novel
Single molecule: bispecific antibody targeting two tumor antigens plus cytotoxic payload. Iza-bren (EGFR×HER3, DXd payload) showed 85.7% confirmed PRs in EGFR ex20ins NSCLC Phase 1. Addresses antigen heterogeneity and may overcome single-antigen-targeted resistance simultaneously.
HIGH INTEREST
PRMT5 Inhibitors
73 mentions
Exploits synthetic lethality in MTAP-deleted tumors. MTAP deletion occurs in roughly 15% of all cancers, creating a metabolic vulnerability that PRMT5 inhibition targets selectively. The biomarker gives clean patient selection. Multiple Phase 1/2 programs active at AACR 2026.
RISING
Pan-RAS Degraders
13 KRAS degrader abstracts
Combining KRAS inhibition with molecular degradation approaches. The hypothesis: elimination of the protein removes the feedback reactivation mechanism that limits catalytic inhibition. G12D and G12V alleles are the primary targets. Still early, but structurally differentiated from inhibitor programs.
EARLY WATCH
mRNA / LNP Oncology
164 mentions
mRNA/LNP applications at AACR 2026 extend past vaccines: mRNA encoding cytokines, tumor-suppressor proteins, and CAR constructs for in vivo T-cell engineering. The COVID manufacturing scale-up created infrastructure oncology programs can now access. Organ-selective LNP delivery is the remaining hard problem for solid tumor applications.
INFRASTRUCTURE
STING Agonists (rebased)
32 mentions
After several failed systemic STING agonist programs, the field has rebased around intratumoral injection and ADC-delivered STING payloads. The combination with checkpoint blockade is the primary clinical hypothesis. Lower systemic exposure addresses the dose-limiting toxicity that killed the first generation.

Signal 04

Resistance Research Is the Next Program Factory

558 resistance mechanism abstracts. Every major approved drug class at AACR 2026 has a parallel resistance research program running. The companies systematically mapping resistance mechanisms for drugs that will become standard of care in two to three years are building the next wave.

T-DXd resistance in HER2+ breast. As T-DXd approaches 1L status, resistance mechanisms are being mapped in parallel. Topoisomerase I mutations, MDR1 upregulation, HER2 heterogeneity, and BRCA reactivation are the leading mechanisms. The biparatopic HER2 ADC programs at AACR 2026 are explicitly designed as T-DXd resistance solutions.

Osimertinib resistance in EGFRm NSCLC. C797S mutation, MET amplification, histologic transformation to SCLC, and KRAS acquisition are the four major mechanisms. HER3-DXd addresses the pan-ErbB bypass. The post-osi resistance market is large, well-characterized, and currently underserved by approved agents.

CDK4/6 inhibitor resistance in ER+ breast. ESR1 mutations drive roughly 40% of resistance to CDK4/6 plus AI combinations. Next-gen ER degraders (elacestrant, camizestrant, giredestrant) are now entering combination studies with CDK4/6 and PI3K/AKT inhibitors in this population.

Methodology

How This Analysis Was Built

3,489 AACR 2026 poster and presentation PDFs processed by a Gemma-4 AI model. Each abstract was structured into company/institution, program, mechanism, indication, modality, key data, and clinical stage, plus a list of genes and molecular targets mentioned. Processing completed April 30, 2026.

Modality, indication, and theme counts use keyword matching on the structured summaries. Theme counts use exact-phrase or word-boundary matching to avoid substring false positives (e.g., "ATM" as a substring matches thousands of non-biology uses; "AI" as a substring matches "brain", "again", etc.). All target counts are per-abstract (distinct abstracts mentioning the target), not total text occurrences. The AML/Leukemia count is anomalously high because it captures broad leukemia biology research using leukemic cell lines.

Academic versus industry classification is estimated from institution name parsing and has a known false positive rate. Stage classification reflects stated intent in the abstract, not independent validation. Coverage data from F-Prime Box deal memos sourced via MCP search, May 2026.
