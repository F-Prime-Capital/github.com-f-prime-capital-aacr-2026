SCLC
353 abstracts
ACCELERATING

SCLC went from a deadzone to one of the most active indications in two years. The catalyst was DLL3, and the pattern at AACR 2026 mirrors what happened to CD19 in lymphoma in 2016: a validated target, multiple modalities converging, and an unmet need large enough to support all of them.

Tarlatamab (BiTE) validated the target clinically (ASCO 2024/2025 data). ZL-1310 ADC followed with 68% ORR in Phase 1, including 80% response in patients with brain metastases. CAR-T programs (AFNT-211, LB2102) and a trispecific (ZG006, DLL3/CD3/DLL3, ORR 78.6% at 30mg) are active. IMforte (ASCO 2025) added a lurbinectedin maintenance option in 1L. The field has gone from no actionable surface antigen to three modalities converging on DLL3 in under three years.

The remaining scientific questions are duration of response, brain metastasis activity, and whether the neuroendocrine expression pattern extends meaningfully to large-cell neuroendocrine carcinoma and extrapulmonary SCLC.

Pancreatic
250 abstracts
FIRST CRACKS

Fourteen years of flatlined OS at 11.1 months. 1,924 ASCO abstracts. Still the same bar. AACR 2026 is the first year where the research volume feels like it is aimed at coherent biology rather than incremental combinations of established agents.

KRAS G12D programs are the primary hope. G12D is present in roughly 90% of PDAC, compared to G12C's near-zero share. MRTX113, GFH375, and TSN16 are the leading G12D programs. Revolution Medicines' pan-KRAS daraxonrasib is in Phase 3 (RASolute 302, announced ASCO 2025) and directly addresses the feedback reactivation problem that limited G12C inhibitors. KRAS degrader abstracts (13 at AACR) represent an early but structurally different approach to the same biology.

CLDN18.2 ADC programs (IBI343, ASKB589) show ORR signals in CLDN18.2-high patients, which represent roughly 40% of PDAC. CDH17 is emerging as a GI-specific ADC target with strong preclinical rationale. Neither is a single-agent cure, but the combination of KRAS inhibition with targeted surface antigen approaches is the most credible near-term hypothesis for moving the OS bar.

Breast
568 abstracts
MATURE ECOSYSTEM

The most research-active solid tumor at AACR 2026. The indication has splintered into well-characterized subtypes, each with its own drug development logic. HER2-high is a T-DXd story with new competitors emerging. HER2-low expanded the targetable population from roughly 20% to 75% of breast cancer patients. TNBC has sacituzumab govitecan and IO combinations. ER-positive late-line disease is increasingly defined by ESR1 mutation status.

The biparatopic HER2 ADC programs at AACR 2026 — JSKN003 (ORR 67.9%), IBI354, TQB2102 (neoadjuvant pCR 76.9% in chemo-free setting) — are explicitly designed around T-DXd resistance. This is the clearest example of the resistance-to-next-program pattern: T-DXd is becoming standard of care and its resistance mechanisms are already being mapped into the next generation.

ESR1 mutations in ER-positive, HER2-negative disease are the next selection biomarker. Companies that combine CDK4/6 inhibitor resistance mechanisms with ESR1-targeted approaches are building in a large and well-characterized patient population.

GBM
195 abstracts
ENGINEERING FRONTIER

The Stupp protocol has been unchanged for 20 years. GBM research volume at AACR 2026 is high relative to the clinical progress, because the field keeps generating interesting early biology without converting it to approvals. The BBB and immunosuppressive microenvironment remain the two structural limits.

Two approaches are gaining real traction. Local delivery of CAR-T (intracranial infusion) partially solves the BBB trafficking problem. EGFRvIII CAR-T with bystander killing (CARv3-TEAM-E) is mechanistically interesting because it addresses antigen heterogeneity — EGFRvIII-negative neighboring cells get killed via the TEAM component. B7-H3 is the other thread: high expression in GBM, pan-solid-tumor distribution, and increasingly multiple modalities targeting it.

The honest assessment is that no Phase 3 catalyst is visible in the near term. The AACR abstracts represent genuine scientific progress that may take another five years to convert to clinical data meaningful enough for approval decisions.

CRC
451 abstracts
STRATIFIED

Colorectal research volume is high but the progress is uneven across subtypes. MSI-H/dMMR (~15% of patients) is a solved problem with IO. BRAF V600E (~10%) has BRAF/MEK combinations. KRAS-mutant (~45%) now has KRAS inhibitor programs for the first time.

The large unmet need is MSS/pMMR colorectal, which represents the majority of patients and does not respond to IO. CDH17 is the most interesting new CRC target at AACR 2026: GI-specific expression, limited normal tissue, and multiple early ADC programs. The KRAS plus IO combination data from KRYSTAL-7 (adagrasib plus pembrolizumab, ORR 44.3% in NSCLC) raises the question of whether the same combination has signal in KRAS-mutant CRC, where co-mutation patterns with STK11 and SMAD4 are different.

CEA CAR-T programs also appear in the CRC-relevant data at AACR 2026 with early ORR data (47%) and 13-month persistence — a persistence metric that distinguishes it from earlier solid tumor CAR-T attempts.

NSCLC
362 abstracts
RESISTANCE FRONTIER

NSCLC has the most stratified patient landscape of any solid tumor, and the research at AACR 2026 reflects that. Osimertinib resistance in EGFRm disease is the dominant scientific question: C797S mutation, MET amplification, histologic transformation to SCLC, and KRAS acquisition are the four major resistance mechanisms, each requiring a different approach. HER3-DXd (patritumab deruxtecan) addresses the pan-ErbB bypass mechanism and is the most advanced post-osi program.

KRAS G12C represents roughly 13% of NSCLC, and the KRYSTAL-7 combination data from ASCO 2025 (ORR 44.3%, mPFS 11.0 months) is the clearest first-line KRAS inhibitor signal in any tumor. MARIPOSA (ESMO 2023/ASCO 2024) validated the bispecific-TKI format (amivantamab plus lazertinib). The bispecific ADC format (iza-bren, EGFR×HER3, 85.7% cPR in ex20ins per AACR 2026 abstract) is the most novel data point from AACR 2026 in this indication.

Prostate
197 abstracts
RADIOPHARM WAVE

PSMA gets 110 mentions across all abstracts at AACR 2026. PLUVICTO validated the radioligand format. The research question now is: what comes after PSMA? PSMA-targeting ADC and bispecific programs are active. The PSMA expression in tumor neovasculature outside prostate is underexplored and may enable expansion to other solid tumors entirely.

Beyond radioligands, BRCA and HRR-selected prostate patients receiving PARP inhibitors represent a maturing subgroup with good biomarker clarity. The CDK12 alteration population, which lacks BRCA mutation but shows DNA damage response dependency, is an active research target at AACR 2026 with a less developed drug set.

AR splice variants (AR-V7) in late-line CRPC remain an unmet need. No approved agent specifically addresses AR-V7-mediated resistance, and multiple academic programs at AACR 2026 are characterizing the mechanisms.

HCC / Liver
133 abstracts
CONSOLIDATING

HCC has moved from a single sorafenib option to an IO-plus-anti-VEGF standard of care in 1L (atezolizumab plus bevacizumab, durvalumab plus tremelimumab). The OS bar has roughly doubled from 2015 to 2025 (based on ASCO-reported landmark data). AACR 2026 research reflects a field that is now asking post-IO questions rather than validating the original IO signal.

GPC3 is the most active HCC-specific surface antigen, with multiple CAR-T and ADC programs in early development. Liver tropism of IV-administered CAR-T may actually be an advantage in HCC: cells that traffic to the liver encounter the tumor directly. This is a different delivery logic than other solid tumors and may explain the disproportionate early clinical interest.

The TIM-3 checkpoint data from AACR/ASCO 2025 (cobolimab plus dostarlimab, ORR 42.9% in Phase 2) is a single-tumor signal in a small cohort — the most credible post-PD-1 checkpoint result in HCC, but not generalizable yet.

Endometrial
65 abstracts
UNDERSERVED

Endometrial cancer is the most common gynecologic malignancy in the US, and it is receiving less research attention than its incidence and mortality burden warrant. 65 abstracts at AACR 2026 versus 568 for breast and 362 for NSCLC. The ratio is not proportional to unmet need.

The AACR data shows concentrated activity around CDK4/6 inhibition in ER-positive endometrial, PARP inhibition in BRCA-mutant/HRR-deficient disease, and FRα ADC programs (folate receptor alpha is highly expressed in endometrial). Endoxifen plus CDK4/6 inhibitor (Mayo Clinic, Atossa Therapeutics) was one of the lead abstracts: combination of ER modulator and CDK4/6 inhibition in ER-positive endometrial, building on a mechanism that already has NCCN guideline status in combination with letrozole.

The patient selection opportunity here is significant. ER-positive endometrial is a sizable and molecularly tractable population that has not received the same ADC or CDK4/6 investment as ER-positive breast cancer, despite sharing the core biology.
